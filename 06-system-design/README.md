## Why This Section Matters

System design interviews are a critical differentiator for mid-to-senior level roles. Interviewers evaluate your ability to:
- Think at scale and reason about trade-offs
- Design distributed, resilient systems
- Apply patterns like CQRS, event sourcing, and saga
- Translate business requirements into technical architecture
- Communicate design decisions clearly under time pressure

## Study Tips

1. Practice drawing system diagrams (Excalidraw, draw.io) — communication is half the interview.
2. Always start with requirements: functional, non-functional (availability, latency, throughput).
3. Estimate scale before jumping to design: QPS, storage, bandwidth.
4. Learn the standard patterns deeply (rather than memorizing every system) — most real problems are variations.
5. After designing, proactively discuss trade-offs and what you would do differently.
